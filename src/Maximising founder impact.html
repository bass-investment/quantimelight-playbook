          Maximising founder impact
          •   4 Minutes to read 
            Article Summary
            Share feedback    
             
            Thanks for sharing your feedback!
            Revolut is a case study of maintaining speed of execution in the face of growing complexity. In 7 years, Revolut became the account of choice for more than 25 million users across 36 countries, with a complete product offering addressing both business and retail clients. Despite the complexity of having 6,000+ employees and dozens of business verticals, Revolut has been releasing products faster and faster.
            Revolut has been launching 11 new products a year since 2020, after many of the initiatives outlined in this playbook were launched, compared to 4 a year before their introduction.
            Key to this success has been Nik Storonskys structured approach to maximising his impact as founder. He remains extremely close to the details of the business, yet he can snooze his notifications when taking time off.
            Our approach to maximising his impact
            The key steps that helped increase the founders impact have been:
            1. Setting up a CEO Office. The CEO Office is a department of top generalists reporting to the founder, with a focus on process design. The CEO Office helped increase the founders impact by
              • Designing the key processes used by the company to scale (performance management, hiring, culture etc.)
              • Project-managing initiatives or task forces overseen directly by the founder
              • Delegating a member to the founder's recurring meetings to help with monitoring and circulating info
            2. Using highly structured and repeatable playbooks for your meetings. Defining and enforcing a repeatable structure for his meetings was key to help Nik monitor company-wide performance. Despite having 30+ executives reporting to him and reviewing every UX change to Revoluts product, he can still keep the pulse of the business and take time to disconnect. He has detailed playbooks for three meeting types, which you can read about separately:
              • One-to-one meetings - to monitor and improve executives' performance
              • Business review meetings - to keep a full view on how well the company tracks against its goals
              • Product review meetings - to review any changes made to the product
            3. Establishing special troubleshooting task forces to resolve any issues that the monitoring meetings identify. This concept helped solve urgent problems, like when the app required a complete redesign to better support long-term product vision, or when the recruitment org needed new processes to support company growth plans. We developed a detailed playbook to structure and run these initiatives, called Project More, which you can read about in a dedicated section of this document.
            Driving principle: stay close to the details
            The three steps all come from Niks basic approach of maintaining a clear understanding of all their organisations domains. This goes against popular VC advice for founders to hire expensive experts with previous scale-up experience and give up control of business processes to them.
            Complete oversight of the business has several advantages:
            • You can anticipate problems before they snowball into an issue that is too late to stop
            • You can jump into details to deal with red flags immediately
            • You dont have to rely on outside help if, and remain in control when things go wrong
            • You maintain quality control by enforcing and monitoring the same metrics across all company departments and, ultimately...
            You maximise your time input as founder  if executives follow a shared framework, you can delegate without the need for you to micro-manage.
            Set up a CEO Office
            The CEO Office is a separate department with a focus on process design and adoption, whose project leads report to the CEO. Its made up of top former startup operators, consultants and investment bankers.
            The CEO Office expands the impact of the founder by:
            • Driving major special projects across the company, such as setting up company-wide processes, restructuring departments or launching new products.
              • Early on, the CEO Office designed many of the company management processes that helped Nik scale the company (performance management, hiring, KPI and goal setting etc).
            • Monitoring the performance of the departments reporting to the CEO:
              • Ensure that best practices are followed
              • Oversee hiring and retention practices
              • Attend one-to-ones and business review meetings
              • Track short-term KPIs
            If you are interested in setting up a CEO Office, see the CEO Office & Chief of Staff playbook.
            Structured playbooks to monitor the company
            Introduce and enforce a structure for each of your review meetings. Benefits include
            • Consistency, and fewer errors or redundant steps
            • Time savings
            • Transparency through shared processes and terminology
            • Foundation for continuous improvement
            Group similar meetings on the same day to minimise context-switching.
            Recurring meetingPurposeBest practices
            One-to-OnesCoach your executives and monitor their performance.
            • Track direct reports' personal KPIs and roadmaps in a project management tool, and make their updates drive the meeting
            • Time-box these meetings to 15 minutes
            Business review meetingsGet a complete and transparent view of current company developments.
            • Track every relevant metric for each department and team in one place (at Revolut, Nik uses PeopleOps, a soon-to-publicly-launch internal platform)
            • Focus on KPIs and roadmap updates during the meeting
            • Check for red flags
            Product review meetingsAlign on design change proposals that impact product experience.
            • Have POs and designers use the same checklist when preparing Figma design prototypes
            • Review low-fidelity prototypes to set direction for development
            • Approve high-fidelity designs that meet your design framework standards
            Establish special troubleshooting task forces
            If any one-to-one or business review meeting reveals critical bottlenecks or severe deviations from company goals, the founder will set up a cross-company task force called Project More.
            The task force is made up of strong generalist problem-solvers who coordinate subject matter experts across the company to solve the issue at hand.
            On a weekly basis, the task force will present to the founder in a Project More meeting, where the founder will:
            • Have the task force present updates to Project Mores KPIs and roadmap
            • Follow up on of assigned action items
            • Discuss next steps
            Read more about launching and managing a Project More on its dedicated page.
